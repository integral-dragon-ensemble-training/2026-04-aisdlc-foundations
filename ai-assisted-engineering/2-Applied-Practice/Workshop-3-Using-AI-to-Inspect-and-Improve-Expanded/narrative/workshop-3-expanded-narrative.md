# Workshop 3 Expanded Narrative

Workshop 2 identified friction. Workshop 3 teaches participants how to aim AI at that friction without letting it sprawl.

The instructor should frame this workshop as a shift from broad prompting to directed engineering investigation. The participant chooses one friction item, turns it into an inspection question, and asks Claude Code to collect evidence.

The most important behavior is prompt tightening. If Claude gives a broad or confident answer, the participant asks for paths, call sites, tests, failure modes, and uncertainty. If Claude suggests a large refactor, the participant asks for three smaller options.

By the end, participants should have one candidate improvement on their branch and a review packet explaining what was changed, why, how it was validated, and what remains unproven.

The bridge into Workshop 4 is direct: Workshop 3 creates candidate changes. Workshop 4 teaches how to decide whether those changes are trustworthy.
