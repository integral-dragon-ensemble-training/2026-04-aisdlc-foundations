# Workshop 1 Expanded Flow Talk Track

Use this with `workshop-1-expanded-flow.excalidraw`.

## Fast Walkthrough

1. Course resources live in `aisdlc-resources`, including Claude Support, Workshop 1 Expanded, and the `AISDLC-tech-diagrams` skill.
2. Participants start Claude Code and provide their first and last name, target repo, and local `course-work` repo path.
3. Claude creates two work areas: a participant branch in the real target repo and a coursework folder under `course-work/participant-work/<FirstName LastName>/`.
4. The learning loop is scorecard, baseline scan, architecture/setup/testing deep dives, one small candidate improvement, then rescore and log.
5. Nothing merges during individual work. The team compares branches, scorecards, evidence, and rescore deltas in group review.

## What To Emphasize

- Claude is a teaching assistant and evidence collector, not the final judge.
- Branches are candidate work areas, not merge instructions.
- Coursework artifacts make the participant's reasoning reviewable.
- The group decides what to merge, rewrite, or discard.
- Internal folders like `_pipeline`, `_instructor-cheat-sheet`, and `__research` do not go to ADO.
