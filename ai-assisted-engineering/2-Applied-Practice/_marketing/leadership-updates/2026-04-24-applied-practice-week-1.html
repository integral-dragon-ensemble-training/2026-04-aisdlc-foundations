How Applied Practice works in front of participants

Every workshop follows the same shape. It's deliberately repeatable — the muscle we're building is the loop, not any single exercise.

Step 01

Define good

Participants start from a shared definition of project health — not opinions.

Step 02

Inspect

Claude Code gathers evidence against the definition, from their actual codebase.

Step 03

Score

Humans — not AI — decide what the evidence means and how to score it.

Step 04

Improve

One small, reviewable change is made — validated by tests and review.

Step 05

Rescore

Run the scorecard again. Capture the delta. Move to the next priority.

Guardrails we teach on day one
  • Claude collects evidence. Humans own scoring and improvement decisions.
  • Improvements must be small enough to review — no speculative rewrites.
  • Every improvement is validated before it counts.

Quality Signals Assessment Module

Running in parallel to the workshop build: a module that turns what the workshops teach into an ongoing quality read on any codebase. Designed to produce signals, not scoreboards.

What it is

A module — not a dashboard product

The module gathers evidence from a repository and its delivery systems, and surfaces stable patterns: which files always show up in rework, which cycle-time segments are dragging, which review-discipline habits are slipping. The output can be rendered as a report, a dashboard, or fed into an existing tool.

Why signals, not scores

Less political, less gameable

Scores invite argument about what to count. Signals report what the codebase is doing, with every value sourced to a specific query. A signal that recurs across two consecutive windows is treated differently than a one-off — that consistency is the headline, not any single number.

Where this sits in the program

Workshops teach the practice. The Quality Signals module is the artifact that lets a team continue the practice after the workshops end — and lets leadership see whether the investment in engineering health is actually paying off, without asking engineers for self-reported metrics.

The posture we're taking
  • Every number resolves to an ADO query, a git command, or a scheduled scan — not to an opinion.
  • Signals that recur across two consecutive windows are treated as structural. Single-window findings are flagged as tentative.
  • No dollar / cost conversions in the first run. Those come later, once the signals themselves are trusted.
