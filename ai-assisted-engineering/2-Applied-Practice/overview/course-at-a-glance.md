# Course at a Glance

Five sessions, sixty minutes each, delivered across one week. The pattern is consistent so you always know what to expect: a short concept framing, examples drawn from real codebases, an exercise, and a debrief.

## The arc of the week

1. **Define what good looks like.** Establish a shared target for project health before discussing anything about AI.
2. **Identify the debt that blocks it.** Treat technical debt as friction that makes every change more expensive — not just messy code.
3. **Use AI to inspect and improve.** Apply targeted, reviewable AI techniques to existing codebases.
4. **Validate what you've changed.** Tests, observability, and security are part of change safety.
5. **Make it repeatable.** Turn cleanup into a team habit instead of a one-off project.

---

## Session 1 — What Good Looks Like

**Theme.** Define software project health in practical terms.

**What you'll explore.**

- Why "clean code" is too narrow a definition of a healthy project.
- Nine categories of project health: documentation, architecture, setup, testability, release discipline, data, observability, security, and workflow.
- How to use a lightweight scorecard to assess a real repository.

**What you'll produce.** A completed health scorecard for a working codebase — yours or a provided example — that names the top areas where change is hardest today.

---

## Session 2 — Technical Debt as Friction

**Theme.** See technical debt as the repeated cost your system charges on every change.

**What you'll explore.**

- A working definition of technical debt that covers more than just code.
- Nine debt categories: knowledge, design, environment, quality confidence, release, data, observability, security, and workflow.
- The "interest" concept — how debt compounds through repeated use.
- How to prioritize debt by its impact on change, confidence, and flow.

**What you'll produce.** A friction map and debt inventory for a codebase, with the highest-interest items called out for targeted improvement.

---

## Session 3 — Using AI to Inspect and Improve

**Theme.** Apply AI as a disciplined engineering tool — to inspect, explain, plan, scaffold, and clean up.

**What you'll explore.**

- Where AI adds the most value in an existing codebase, and where it overreaches.
- Prompt patterns that produce useful analysis instead of generic noise.
- Practical techniques for documentation gaps, architecture mapping, and targeted test improvement.
- A shared prompt template you can reuse on your own projects.

**What you'll produce.** AI-generated outputs you've reviewed and validated: a documentation gap list, an architecture map, and a set of test improvement ideas.

---

## Session 4 — Validation: Tests, Observability, Security

**Theme.** Confidence beats raw speed. An improvement only counts if you can validate it.

**What you'll explore.**

- Why coverage is a count and confidence is a capability.
- Observability as part of maintainability — logs, metrics, and traces as feedback mechanisms for change.
- Security and supply-chain checks as baseline validation, not optional polish.
- Validation gates for AI-assisted changes.

**What you'll produce.** An observability gap review and a completed AI-assisted refactoring review — evaluating whether a proposed change is safe to merge.

---

## Session 5 — Making It Repeatable

**Theme.** Turn one-off cleanup into normal engineering practice.

**What you'll explore.**

- Staged modernization instead of rewrite thinking.
- What to standardize in team workflow, review norms, and AI usage.
- How to build a backlog and cadence that keeps improvement work moving.

**What you'll produce.** A 30-day improvement plan for your own team — three quick wins, two structural improvements, one workflow change, and one measurement change.

---

## What every session looks like

Each 60-minute session follows the same rhythm:

- **0–10 min** — concept framing
- **10–25 min** — examples and repo inspection
- **25–45 min** — exercise or small-group activity
- **45–55 min** — debrief and synthesis
- **55–60 min** — next-day setup
