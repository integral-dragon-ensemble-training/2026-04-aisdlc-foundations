# How to Prepare

A short guide to get the most out of the Applied Practice week.

## What to bring

- **A laptop** with your normal development environment set up.
- **An AI coding assistant you already use** — Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot, or equivalent. If you're not sure which to bring, ask your facilitator before the first session.
- **A codebase you know well.** Ideally a real project from your team. A public repository you contribute to works too. You'll return to it in several exercises, so pick one you're comfortable inspecting openly in small-group discussion.

If you can't bring a codebase you own, your facilitator will have example repositories ready.

## A few minutes of pre-work

Before Session 1, spend ten minutes with the codebase you're bringing and jot down answers to these questions:

1. How long does it take a new engineer to go from clone to a working local environment?
2. What's one change your team has avoided making because it felt too risky or too expensive?
3. When something breaks in production, how quickly can you tell what happened?

You won't be asked to share these publicly. They're a personal baseline you'll compare against what you learn during the week.

## Set expectations for yourself

This week is practical. You'll do more inspecting, scoring, and planning than you will writing new code. That's intentional — the premise of the course is that most engineering teams are blocked by friction, not by typing speed.

A few things to keep in mind:

- **Bring judgment, not just tools.** The exercises are designed to build a habit of evaluating AI outputs rather than accepting them.
- **Small, validated improvements beat large speculative rewrites.** You'll practice scoping changes you can actually verify.
- **The outputs are for you.** The scorecards, maps, and plans you produce are working documents for your own team, not assignments to be graded.

## What to do between sessions

Each session ends with a short next-day setup. Plan on fifteen to thirty minutes between sessions to review the day's artifact, skim the suggested reading, and bring one observation or question to the next day. Nothing more is required.

## If you get stuck

Flag it in session or reach your facilitator between sessions. The week is designed to be responsive — if an exercise isn't landing against your real-world context, we'd rather adjust than push through.
