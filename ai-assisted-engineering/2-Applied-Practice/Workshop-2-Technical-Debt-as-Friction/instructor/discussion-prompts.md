# Workshop 2 — Discussion Prompts

Use these during the Workshop 2 debrief or as warm-ups before the Friction Map exercise. Each prompt is designed to surface specific, operational examples rather than abstract complaints.

## Primary prompts

- **What debt does your team pay interest on every week?**
  Push for specificity — who pays it, how often, how much time. If the answer is vague, the debt is not yet diagnosed.

- **What is treated as "normal friction" today that should actually be considered technical debt?**
  This surfaces the invisible tax — the things that have become so routine nobody flags them anymore.

- **Which debt category hurts morale the most? Which hurts delivery the most?**
  Often these are different items. Morale-hitting debt (cognitive load, exhausting workflows) is worth tracking, but delivery-hitting debt usually deserves higher priority in a 30-day plan.

## Follow-up prompts

- What debt in your environment is deliberate, and what is accidental?
- Which of the nine categories does your team under-invest in the most?
- What would it cost to stop paying this specific item of interest for one month?
- Who on your team would disagree with your ranking, and why?
- What is one debt item you could classify more precisely now than you could yesterday?

## Exit prompt

*"Which kind of debt in your environment creates the highest repeated tax?"*

Ask for one sentence, written down. This becomes the bridge into the next workshop, where AI-assisted inspection gets pointed at the participant's actual highest-interest debt.
