# Workshop 2 Expanded Narrative

Workshop 1 asked: what does a healthy project look like?

Workshop 2 asks: what is making this project hard to change?

Participants start with their Workshop 1 scorecard. Low scores are not automatically debt, but they are strong leads. Claude Code helps inspect the target repository and turn those leads into a friction inventory.

The instructor should keep the language concrete. Debt is not "bad code." Debt is repeated cost. A missing setup step is debt if every new engineer loses time. An unclear module boundary is debt if every change requires defensive archaeology. A flaky test is debt if the team stops trusting the pipeline.

Claude's job is to collect evidence and suggest candidate friction items. The participant's job is to decide whether the evidence is real, classify the debt, and estimate interest.

By the end, every participant should have:

- a friction register
- top debt items ranked by interest and flow impact
- one candidate debt-reduction improvement
- a recommendation for group review

The bridge into Workshop 3 is deliberate: Workshop 2 identifies where AI should be aimed. Workshop 3 teaches how to inspect and improve those areas safely.
