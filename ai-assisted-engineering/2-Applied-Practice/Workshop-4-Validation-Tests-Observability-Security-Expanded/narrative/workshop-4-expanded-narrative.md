# Workshop 4 Expanded Narrative

Workshop 3 produced candidate AI-assisted changes. Workshop 4 asks whether those changes are trustworthy.

The instructor should stress that validation is not a ceremony after the real work. Validation is what turns a generated change into an engineering change.

Participants create a validation matrix for their branch. Claude Code helps map the change to evidence: tests, observability, security, dependency checks, and operational verification. The participant decides whether the evidence is sufficient.

The important phrase is "what remains unproven." Even a good change can have unverified risks. The goal is not to block every change. The goal is to make the confidence story explicit.

By the end, participants should be able to say whether their candidate change should be approved, revised, or rejected, and why.

Workshop 5 then turns those validation habits into a repeatable team operating model.
