# Discussion Prompts — Session 5: Making It Repeatable

## Core Day 5 prompts (from research)

- What is one improvement your team could normalize next month?
- Which quality habit should become part of the default workflow?
- What does a realistic 30-day AI-assisted improvement plan look like for your current project?

---

## Opening prompts

- "What would make this work after the class ends?" *(use to anchor the session)*
- If you had one hour a week for engineering improvement, how would you spend it?
- What did your team try to "fix" in the last 12 months that did not stick? Why not?

## Prompts for the *rewrite vs. stage it* discussion

- Which of the situations in the lightweight exercise did your group split on? Why?
- What evidence would make a rewrite defensible for you? What evidence would make "stage it" obvious?
- When does "rewrite" actually mean "we do not understand the code yet"?
- Where is AI genuinely helpful in a rewrite decision, and where is it dangerous?

## Prompts for the 30-day plan

- Why did you put your items in that order?
- If you could only keep three of your seven items, which three?
- Who is the realistic owner for each item — not the aspirational one?
- What is your visible win in week one? If there is no answer, the plan needs a quick win.
- How will you know a structural improvement is "done" instead of "started"?
- What would make your 30-day check-in trustworthy — not just a meeting?

## Prompts for workflow standardization

- Which of your current PR norms are real, and which are theater?
- If you adopted Conventional Commits next Monday, who would push back and why?
- What is the lightest possible PR template that would still catch AI-assisted drift?
- What CI check would have caught the last thing that went wrong in production?
- What is one review norm you tolerate that you would not teach to a new hire?

## Prompts for measurement

- Which of the metrics on the "what to measure" slide would actually change a decision in your team?
- Which metric is easy to game? What behavior would it accidentally reward?
- What does your team already measure that is mostly vanity?
- What would "onboarding friction" look like as a number in your environment?

## Prompts for AI discipline

- What AI-assisted task in your team still needs tighter workflow norms?
- Where has AI output been accepted in your codebase because it was fluent, not because it was evidence-backed?
- What is one AI-assisted task your team should standardize a prompt for, instead of improvising each time?
- What review rule would you set for AI-assisted PRs that you do not already enforce?

## Closing prompts

- In one sentence: what change would you personally normalize in the next 30 days?
- Which of the five weekly takeaways would your team resist most, and why?
- If you had to explain the week to a skeptical engineer in 60 seconds, what would you say?
- What is the one thing from this week you want your team to treat as normal, not special?
