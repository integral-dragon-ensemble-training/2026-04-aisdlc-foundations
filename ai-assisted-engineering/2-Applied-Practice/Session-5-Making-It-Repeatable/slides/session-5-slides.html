SESSION 05 | WORKFLOW
INTEGRAL DRAGON

What to standardize in team workflow

  • →Repo health scorecard reviews · run on a regular cadence, not only when something breaks.
  • →PR templates for AI-assisted work · require scope, evidence, and validation plan in every change.
  • →Small-diff preference · narrow scope before merge; reject sprawling, speculative diffs.
  • →Baseline checks in CI · tests, lint, security scans, dependency review on every PR.
  • →Periodic debt review · a recurring prioritization session so debt does not silently accumulate.

Governance can be lightweight and still useful. The goal is normal workflow, not a program office.

What to measure

Changeability

Onboarding friction

Time from repo clone to running the system locally or in an isolated environment.

Changeability

Time to first safe change

How long a new engineer takes to land a reviewed, merged PR.

Delivery

Build reliability

Share of CI runs that go green without retries or flake-driven reruns.

Confidence

Test stability

Flake rate on main. Tests that fail randomly teach the team to ignore signal.

Operations

Deployment verification

Time from deploy to "known healthy" — not just "pipeline green".

Operations

Incident diagnosis speed

Time from alert to probable cause. Observability pays off here.

Also track recurring friction categories — what keeps coming up in retros. Tie every metric to practical usefulness; avoid vanity.

SESSION 05 | 30-DAY PLAN
INTEGRAL DRAGON

30-day team plan

3 × Quick Wins

Visible, low-risk, fast

README fixes, one pre-commit hook, a broken link purge, a missing runbook — finished inside a week.

2 × Structural

Foundations that compound

Reproducible setup, a real test for the riskiest path, an architecture map, or a schema-migration pattern.

1 × Workflow

One norm everyone adopts

New PR template, small-diff rule, AI-assisted review checklist, or Conventional Commits adoption.

1 × Validation

One new signal of truth

Deployment verification check, structured logging upgrade, post-deploy smoke test, or dependency scan gate.

Plus one 30-day check-in — rescore the repo, re-rank the debt list, or revisit one chosen metric. Force prioritization. Teams do not need 30 actions; they need the right seven.

SESSION 05 | AI DISCIPLINE
INTEGRAL DRAGON

The mature use of AI in engineering

  • →Use AI to inspect, explain, propose, and standardize — not to generate more code than the team can validate.
  • →Require human review and evidence before any AI-assisted change lands on main.
  • →Treat AI output as draft material with a burden of proof — fluency is not evidence.
  • →Prefer repeatable prompts and checklists over ad-hoc improvisation in every PR.

AI belongs inside disciplined engineering — not as a chaotic side channel that routes around review.
