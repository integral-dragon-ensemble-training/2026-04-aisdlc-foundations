# Day 5 Slide Deck — Making It Repeatable

## Slide 1 — Do Not Turn This Into a Rewrite Fantasy

**Core message:** The goal is staged modernization and repeatable improvement.

**Bullets**
- Most teams cannot rewrite safely or quickly
- Small, compounding improvements beat grand clean-slate plans
- Start where friction is high and validation is possible

**Suggested visual**
- Stair-step roadmap instead of big-bang rewrite diagram

**Speaker notes**
This slide protects students from the most common overreaction.

---

## Slide 2 — A Practical Improvement Sequence

**Core message:** There is a sensible order for paying down debt.

**Bullets**
- Make the system visible
- Fix setup and documentation blockers
- Increase confidence with tests and data discipline
- Improve observability and release visibility
- Standardize workflow and review norms

**Suggested visual**
- Five-step sequence with arrows

**Speaker notes**
This is the operationalized version of the course thesis.

---

## Slide 3 — What to Standardize in Team Workflow

**Core message:** Repeatable practice needs explicit norms.

**Bullets**
- Repo health scorecard reviews
- PR templates for AI-assisted work
- Small-diff preference
- Baseline checks in CI
- Periodic debt review and prioritization

**Suggested visual**
- Checklist or policy card visual

**Speaker notes**
Show that governance can be lightweight and still useful.

---

## Slide 4 — What to Measure

**Core message:** Measure outcomes tied to changeability and confidence, not vanity.

**Bullets**
- Onboarding friction
- Time to first safe change
- Build reliability
- Test stability
- Deployment verification speed
- Incident diagnosis speed
- Recurring friction categories

**Suggested visual**
- Measurement dashboard mockup

**Speaker notes**
Avoid making this feel like bureaucracy. Tie every metric to practical usefulness.

---

## Slide 5 — 30-Day Team Plan

**Core message:** Students should leave with an executable near-term plan.

**Bullets**
- Three quick wins
- Two structural improvements
- One workflow change
- One validation improvement
- One way to re-check progress in 30 days

**Suggested visual**
- Simple 30-day roadmap

**Speaker notes**
Force prioritization. Teams do not need 30 actions; they need the right 7.

---

## Slide 6 — The Mature Use of AI in Engineering

**Core message:** AI should become part of disciplined engineering, not a chaotic side channel.

**Bullets**
- Use AI to inspect, explain, propose, and standardize
- Require human review and evidence
- Treat AI output as draft material
- Prefer repeatable prompts and checklists over improvisation

**Suggested visual**
- Human + AI operating model diagram

**Speaker notes**
This is the closing philosophy slide.

---

## Slide 7 — Final Takeaways

**Core message:** Students should leave with a compact set of principles.

**Bullets**
- Good engineering makes AI more useful
- Technical debt is friction that compounds
- AI is strongest in understanding and improvement work
- Validation turns plausible output into trustworthy change
- Small repeatable improvements beat heroic cleanups

**Suggested visual**
- Five takeaway cards or a final synthesis graphic

**Speaker notes**
End with the line: 'The point is not more code. The point is easier change.'
