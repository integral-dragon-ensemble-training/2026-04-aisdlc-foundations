# Course Phrasing and Terminology

## Strong framing lines

- “The point is not more code. The point is easier change.”
- “Good engineering fundamentals make AI more useful.”
- “AI amplifies clarity, but it also amplifies confusion.”
- “Technical debt is the repeated tax paid when the system resists change.”
- “If you cannot validate the change, AI only helped you move faster in the dark.”

## Ways to explain technical debt in the age of AI

- “AI makes hidden debt more visible, but it can also build on top of it faster.”
- “A bad codebase plus AI does not become a good codebase. It becomes a faster bad codebase.”
- “AI lowers the cost of generating code. It does not automatically lower the cost of understanding, validating, operating, or evolving that code.”

## Good terms for “what good looks like”

- Project health
- Changeability
- Engineering readiness
- Maintainer fitness
- Operationally trustworthy software
- High-confidence software delivery

## Good terms for the course arc

- From friction to flow
- Seeing and reducing the cost of change
- AI-assisted modernization through staged improvement
- Project health in the age of AI
- Paying down technical debt with AI-assisted engineering

## Strong ways to explain observability

- “Observability is part of maintainability.”
- “A system that cannot explain itself is hard to change safely.”
- “Logs, metrics, and traces are not just ops tools; they are feedback mechanisms for change.”

## Strong ways to explain testing

- “Coverage is a count. Confidence is a capability.”
- “Tests matter because they support change, not because they exist.”
- “A useful test suite helps you move. A brittle one only makes you careful.”

## Strong ways to explain reproducibility

- “If the team cannot set up the project reliably, the project is already resisting change.”
- “Reproducible setup is not polish; it is the foundation of collaborative engineering.”

## Strong ways to explain AI usage discipline

- “Use AI to inspect, explain, propose, and scaffold.”
- “Use humans to decide, validate, and own the change.”
- “Treat AI output as draft material with a burden of proof.”
