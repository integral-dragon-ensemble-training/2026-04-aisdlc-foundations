# Practice and Feedback

## Session 16

## Auditability and Workflow Narration

### In-session practice 01

## Build the narrative

Take an AI-assisted change and write a PR narration that answers:

- what was requested
- what the agent did
- what the human reviewed
- why the chosen approach was accepted
- what trace evidence supports the decision

### In-session practice 02

## Evidence checklist

For one recent AI-assisted task, identify which of the following you could reconstruct later:

- request
- session log
- decision points
- rejected alternatives
- approval or review notes
- trace or event references

Mark anything missing as a gap in auditability.

### Discussion prompts

- What do auditors or incident responders usually ask for that teams fail to preserve?
- When does a PR summary become useful evidence instead of ceremonial text?
- Which part of your workflow is currently the hardest to reconstruct after the fact?

### Between-session fieldwork

Complete all three:

1. Pick one AI-assisted change you can still remember clearly.
2. Write a short narration that would let a stranger understand the decision path.
3. Identify what evidence you would still need to make it audit-ready.

### Submission prompt

Write 6 to 8 sentences answering:

- What part of the workflow was easiest to narrate?
- What evidence was missing or hard to recover?
- Why would a “the AI did it” explanation fail in an enterprise review?

### Survey questions

- Does your team currently preserve enough evidence to reconstruct an AI-assisted change later?
- After this session, how confident are you that you can write a PR narration that survives compliance review?
- Which audit artifact would help your environment most right now: session logs, trace IDs, PR narration, or decision notes?
