- Cohort / team(s): `Ensemble pilot cohort`
- Prepared by: `Integral Dragon`

## Executive Snapshot
- Overall status: `Green`
- This week in one sentence: `The cohort is already actively using AI, and the primary opportunity is to improve reviewability, testing discipline, and safe workflow integration rather than basic awareness.`
- Biggest improvement: `We now have a clear baseline on adoption, blockers, preferred learning modes, and what the cohort wants optimized first.`
- Biggest risk or blocker: `The largest near-term risks are output quality, security/privacy concerns, and uneven workflow fit across tools and governance constraints.`

## 1. Adoption
- AI-assisted workflow used this week: `18 / 26 participants daily (69%)`, with `24 / 26 (92%)` using AI at least several times per week

- Adoption trend vs prior week: `Baseline established`
- Notes: `The cohort is not starting from zero. GitHub Copilot, Claude, Microsoft Copilot, Claude Code, and ChatGPT are already in active use. The enablement need is disciplined operating practice, not tool awareness.`

## 6. High-Signal Examples
- Example win: `We now have a defensible baseline showing that the strongest initial use cases are understanding unfamiliar code, accelerating tests, and debugging/root-cause analysis.`
- Example lesson learned or failure mode: `The cohort is already AI-active, but adoption confidence is more mature technically than culturally. One of the lowest-scoring items was whether AI mistakes would be held against the engineer.`
- Representative artifact(s): `baseline survey analysis`, `training redesign`, `weekly scorecard`, `session materials`, `future PR and lab artifacts`

## 7. Weekly Decision / Ask
- Continue as planned: `Yes`
- Adjust next week’s emphasis toward: `testing`, `reviewability`, `policy`, `tooling`, `codebase understanding`

## Recommended Use
